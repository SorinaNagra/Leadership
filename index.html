Summary of Learning: Nursing leadership

Formal Leadership Positions

  • Charge nurse:

    • Oversees the floor and supports staff: Responsible for managing assignments, assigning breaks, overseeing client flow and safety (Fire alarms, Code whites) (College of Nurses of Ontario [CNO], 2015)

    • “staffing role of charge nurses is critical to the safety of all nurses and patients in the health-care environment” (Registered Nurses’ Association of Ontario [RNAO], 2017, pp. 36)

  • Clinical educator

    • Develops, implements, and evaluates clinical education programs based on evidence-based approaches. Collaborates with team to promote continuity of quality improvement. Supports staff technically and promotes a positive work environment that endorses knowledge integration (Trillium interim staffing, 2021; RNAO, 2013)

  • Nurse manager

    • Nursing and executive roles responsible for staffing, safety and quality assessment, client satisfaction and finances. (Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality, 2012; Bloomberg School of Nursing, 2021; The Ontario Public Service, 2008)

  • Clinical nurse specialist

    • An RN with advanced knowledge and skills that made complex decisions for client care. Work as a consult to advice in discharge planning. (Canadian Nurses Association, 2016)

Personal Strengths

Personal strengths from the RNAO point-of-care leadership examples

  • Personal focus during this program in improving my communication and interpersonal skills

  • Build competence in relationship and trust

    • Focus on ensuring patients feel their health care goals and preferences are addressed and included in their care plans as part of patient- centered care
    • Practice safe and competent care by evaluating clients, implementing care and communicating patient findings.
    • Work collaboratively with interdisciplinary team to address client needs

  • Empowerment

    • Seek out feedback on interactions
    • Direct patients to appropriate resources in their community, provide assistance in navigating their care, provide health teaching documentation to reinforce teaching.
    • Work towards having clients understand their care plan, empower clients by having them feel involved in their care as a partner
    • Support environment that fosters knowledge integration
    • Apply evidence- based practices at the bedside
    • Participate in self- reflection to identify learning needs/ gaps, self- assess quality of care and self- improvement.

  • Leading, supporting and sustaining change

    • Upheld feedback directed at the floor – work towards self- improvement.
    • Advocating for patient needs – coordinating with other members of the team
      • Reporting falls concerns for the home (mechanical falls, unreported hx, without supportive equipment) with physiotherapy) Coordinating with physicians to address concerns of pain (adequate management on discharge) Balancing complexities if the system, managing competing priorities *Seek conformation for professional decisions

#Observations of Charge Nurse Roll - Shadow Day Charge plays a supportive role in the team.

  • Advocates for the needs of the team

  • Scheduled staff for the week

  • Makes recommendations based on seniority to fill staffing requirements on short days.

  • Clear communicator

  • Addresses safety concerns:

  • Organized safety huddle

  • Floor specific concerns: pressure ulcers, falls and delirium, identifies patients that may need additional rounding and ensures staff are supported.

  • Ensure floor is aware of safety concerns.

  • Takes lead in emergency situations (Code Red, etc.)

  • Relay concerns of other members of the interdisciplinary team and identifies strategies to address them. i.e. provided a reminder (previously addressed by the clinical educator at a previous meeting) that the doctors at the fracture clinic have voiced the concern of an increased number of patients returning with maceration due to damp dressings. Relayed concerns to staff and provided example of patient education regarding dressing care.

  • Covers patients when staff are short staffed, or breaks need to be covered in same day surgery.

  • Receptive to feedback

  • Adjust huddle (now at 0800 instead of 0730) in response to feedback.

  • Problem solver: Documents staff concerns to bring to manager, mitigates conflict between staff members and addresses staff concerns. Acts proactively to prevent conflict between family members and the healthcare facility.

  • Encourages continual learning - brings new opportunities to the attention of staff.

  • Bed management: tracks and evaluates all patients currently occupying beds and potential admissions from the emergency department up for evaluation, elective surgeries schedules for the OR and transfers from other units. Creates documented summary and reports up to nurse manager. Makes critical decisions regarding bed management.

    • Is available as a resource when the team needs support.

    • advocates for staffing needs

    • Oversees admissions and discharges.

    • Judgments for potential discharge, demonstrates problem solving and critical thinking, aids in this process by acting as a liaison between the healthcare team, management, other health facilities and communicating with families.

    • Judgments for safe workloads, risk assessment, influence number of Operating rooms

    • Makes suggestions to aid in cost-effective management of the unit.

    • Plans and facilitates the closure of elective beds on the weekends (10 same-day surgery/ arthroplasty beds)
